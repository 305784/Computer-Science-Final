World Cup 2026 Info

Overview

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the largest tournament in soccer history, co-hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada from June 11 to July 19, 2026. It marks a historic milestone by expanding to 48 participating nations competing in 104 total matches across 16 host cities. It all started in 1930 when the first FIFA World Cup was held in Uruguay, featuring just 13 invited teams playing in a single host city. Over nearly a century, the tournament grew steadily from 16 teams to 24, and eventually to 32 teams in 1998. Today, the tournament has evolved from a small, invitation-only event into a massive global spectacle that unifies billions of fans and showcases talent from every corner of the planet.
